## 90-Second Interview Pitch

FinCredit AI started as a way to make beginner stock research more structured. Instead of jumping between a stock page, SEC data, news, spreadsheet notes, and a general chatbot, the app puts the workflow in one place.

The user logs in, searches a ticker, reviews chart/market/SEC/news context, adds stocks to a simulated watchlist or paper portfolio, refreshes prices, records simulated buy/sell transactions, and asks AI questions grounded in their holdings and history. The admin dashboard is read-only and shows user and product activity summaries.

Technically, the frontend is Next.js and TypeScript. The backend is FastAPI with SQLAlchemy and PostgreSQL. Market/news data comes from yfinance, fundamentals come from SEC Company Facts, and the AI workflow is orchestrated with LangGraph and LangChain. Local Ollama is optional because the app has deterministic timeout fallback, which keeps demos stable. I also added Docker support, environment examples, Playwright E2E coverage, screenshot capture, and launch documentation.

The project is paper trading only and not financial advice. The engineering value is in connecting product flow, auth, database design, external data, AI orchestration, graceful fallback behavior, and tests into one coherent app.
